Nsima chambo

Nsima chambo is a traditional Malawian dish that combines the staple food of nsima (a type of maize porridge) with chambo, a popular freshwater fish found in Lake Malawi.
The dish is known for its rich flavors and hearty ingredients.
To prepare nsima chambo, the chambo fish is typically seasoned with a blend of spices and then cooked until tender. The nsima is made by boiling maize flour in water until it thickens to a smooth consistency. The cooked chambo is then served on top of the nsima, creating a delicious and satisfying meal.
Nsima chambo is a beloved dish in Malawi and is often enjoyed during family gatherings and special occasions. It is a great way to experience the unique flavors of Malawian cuisine while enjoying the nutritional benefits of both nsima and chambo.
